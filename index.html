GameHub

During the last two rounds of working on the gamehub site, I never had time to style the “account profile” page. During the process of styling the account profile page, I added javascript to control the tab-navigation. I then applied that javascript to the tab-navigation on the product pages. I also made adjustments to the login, sign-up, confirmation, and contact pages.

I was never quite happy with the header on this site, it was super wonky when it was resized, so I fixed that. Since the navigation is so simple I got rid of the drop-nav and kept it visible at every size and adjusted the search bar as well. I also updated the background image so it was less pixelated and the logo so the tagline is visible.

On the product page I adjusted the layout and got rid of the price card. In the end it was just too busy, and I felt a simpler approach was best.

Community Science Museum

For the improvements to this site, I started by adding javascript where appropriate. This includes the opening hours on the top of the site. Now they will update dynamically and display the day’s hours. I also added javascript for navigation control, and subscribe and form validation.

For non-javascript changes, referenced the feedback I received on the project to make the image banners stretch across the screen and also updated my colors to make them all accessible.

The Actic Berg

I based my improvements for this site on the feedback I received. I added font-family style to the paragraph tags and some of the heading tags (don’t understand how I managed to leave that off before), I added email validation and made adjustments to the form HTML to not include break tags. I also went through all my images and added alt-text where needed.

I updated the javascript to style the category link for the page the user has clicked on. I also added javascript to dynamically update the page titles for individual blog posts. I also adjusted the javascript to display the primary category and not the “all posts” category on the carousel links and on the individual posts. I attempted to hide the carousel buttons, but didn’t quite figure it out in time.
